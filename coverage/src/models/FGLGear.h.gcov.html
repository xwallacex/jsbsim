      62             : /** Landing gear model.
      63             :     Calculates forces and moments due to landing gear reactions. This is done in
      64             :     several steps, and is dependent on what kind of gear is being modeled. Here
      65             :     are the parameters that can be specified in the config file for modeling
      66             :     landing gear:
      67             :     <p>
      68             :     <h3>Physical Characteristics</h3>
      69             :     <ol>
      70             :     <li>X, Y, Z location, in inches in structural coordinate frame</li>
      71             :     <li>Spring constant, in lbs/ft</li>
      72             :     <li>Damping coefficient, in lbs/ft/sec</li>
      73             :     <li>Dynamic Friction Coefficient</li>
      74             :     <li>Static Friction Coefficient</li>
      75             :     </ol></p><p>
      76             :     <h3>Operational Properties</h3>
      77             :     <ol>
      78             :     <li>Name</li>
      79             :     <li>Brake Group Membership {one of LEFT | CENTER | RIGHT | NOSE | TAIL | NONE}</li>
      80             :     <li>Max Steer Angle, in degrees</li>
      81             :     </ol></p>
      82             :     <p>
      83             :     <h3>Algorithm and Approach to Modeling</h3>
      84             :     <ol>
      85             :     <li>Find the location of the uncompressed landing gear relative to the CG of
      86             :     the aircraft. Remember, the structural coordinate frame that the aircraft is
      87             :     defined in is: X positive towards the tail, Y positive out the right side, Z
      88             :     positive upwards. The locations of the various parts are given in inches in
      89             :     the config file.</li>
      90             :     <li>The vector giving the location of the gear (relative to the cg) is
      91             :     rotated 180 degrees about the Y axis to put the coordinates in body frame (X
      92             :     positive forwards, Y positive out the right side, Z positive downwards, with
      93             :     the origin at the cg). The lengths are also now given in feet.</li>
      94             :     <li>The new gear location is now transformed to the local coordinate frame
      95             :     using the body-to-local matrix. (Mb2l).</li>
      96             :     <li>Knowing the location of the center of gravity relative to the ground
      97             :     (height above ground level or AGL) now enables gear deflection to be
      98             :     calculated. The gear compression value is the local frame gear Z location
      99             :     value minus the height AGL. [Currently, we make the assumption that the gear
     100             :     is oriented - and the deflection occurs in - the Z axis only. Additionally,
     101             :     the vector to the landing gear is currently not modified - which would
     102             :     (correctly) move the point of contact to the actual compressed-gear point of
     103             :     contact. Eventually, articulated gear may be modeled, but initially an
     104             :     effort must be made to model a generic system.] As an example, say the
     105             :     aircraft left main gear location (in local coordinates) is Z = 3 feet
     106             :     (positive) and the height AGL is 2 feet. This tells us that the gear is
     107             :     compressed 1 foot.</li>
     108             :     <li>If the gear is compressed, a Weight-On-Wheels (WOW) flag is set.</li>
     109             :     <li>With the compression length calculated, the compression velocity may now
     110             :     be calculated. This will be used to determine the damping force in the
     111             :     strut. The aircraft rotational rate is multiplied by the vector to the wheel
     112             :     to get a wheel velocity in body frame. That velocity vector is then
     113             :     transformed into the local coordinate frame.</li>
     114             :     <li>The aircraft cg velocity in the local frame is added to the
     115             :     just-calculated wheel velocity (due to rotation) to get a total wheel
     116             :     velocity in the local frame.</li>
     117             :     <li>The compression speed is the Z-component of the vector.</li>
     118             :     <li>With the wheel velocity vector no longer needed, it is normalized and
     119             :     multiplied by a -1 to reverse it. This will be used in the friction force
     120             :     calculation.</li>
     121             :     <li>Since the friction force takes place solely in the runway plane, the Z
     122             :     coordinate of the normalized wheel velocity vector is set to zero.</li>
     123             :     <li>The gear deflection force (the force on the aircraft acting along the
     124             :     local frame Z axis) is now calculated given the spring and damper
     125             :     coefficients, and the gear deflection speed and stroke length. Keep in mind
     126             :     that gear forces always act in the negative direction (in both local and
     127             :     body frames), and are not capable of generating a force in the positive
     128             :     sense (one that would attract the aircraft to the ground). So, the gear
     129             :     forces are always negative - they are limited to values of zero or less. The
     130             :     gear force is simply the negative of the sum of the spring compression
     131             :     length times the spring coefficient and the gear velocity times the damping
     132             :     coefficient.</li>
     133             :     <li>The lateral/directional force acting on the aircraft through the landing
     134             : 
     135             :     gear (along the local frame X and Y axes) is calculated next. First, the
     136             :     friction coefficient is multiplied by the recently calculated Z-force. This
     137             :     is the friction force. It must be given direction in addition to magnitude.
     138             :     We want the components in the local frame X and Y axes. From step 9, above,
     139             :     the conditioned wheel velocity vector is taken and the X and Y parts are
     140             :     multiplied by the friction force to get the X and Y components of friction.
     141             :     </li>
     142             :     <li>The wheel force in local frame is next converted to body frame.</li>
     143             :     <li>The moment due to the gear force is calculated by multiplying r x F
     144             :     (radius to wheel crossed into the wheel force). Both of these operands are
     145             :     in body frame.</li>
     146             :     </ol>
     147             : 
     148             :     <h3>Configuration File Format for \<contact> Section:</h3>
     149             : @code{.xml}
     150             :         <contact type="{BOGEY | STRUCTURE}" name="{string}">
     151             :             <location unit="{IN | M}">
     152             :                 <x> {number} </x>
     153             :                 <y> {number} </y>
     154             :                 <z> {number} </z>
     155             :             </location>
     156             :             <orientation unit="{RAD | DEG}">
     157             :                 <pitch> {number} </pitch>
     158             :                 <roll> {number} </roll>
     159             :                 <yaw> {number} </yaw>
     160             :             </orientation>
     161             :             <static_friction> {number} </static_friction>
     162             :             <dynamic_friction> {number} </dynamic_friction>
     163             :             <rolling_friction> {number} </rolling_friction>
     164             :             <spring_coeff unit="{LBS/FT | N/M}"> {number} </spring_coeff>
     165             :             <damping_coeff type="{ | SQUARE}" unit="{LBS/FT/SEC | N/M/SEC}"> {number} </damping_coeff>
     166             :             <damping_coeff_rebound type="{ | SQUARE}" unit="{LBS/FT/SEC | N/M/SEC}"> {number} </damping_coeff_rebound>
     167             :             <max_steer unit="DEG"> {number | 0 | 360} </max_steer>
     168             :             <brake_group> {NONE | LEFT | RIGHT | CENTER | NOSE | TAIL} </brake_group>
     169             :             <retractable>{0 | 1}</retractable>
     170             :             <table name="{CORNERING_COEFF}" type="internal">
     171             :                 <tableData>
     172             :                     {cornering parameters}
     173             :                 </tableData>
     174             :             </table>
     175             :         </contact>
     176             : @endcode
     177             :     @author Jon S. Berndt
     178             :     @see Richard E. McFarland, "A Standard Kinematic Model for Flight Simulation at
     179             :      NASA-Ames", NASA CR-2497, January 1975
     180             :     @see Barnes W. McCormick, "Aerodynamics, Aeronautics, and Flight Mechanics",
     181             :      Wiley & Sons, 1979 ISBN 0-471-03032-5
     182             :     @see W. A. Ragsdale, "A Generic Landing Gear Dynamics Model for LASRS++",
     183             :      AIAA-2000-4303
